From the time the grape starts growing until the wine is poured into your glass, many factors influence the final
taste of the wine. Generally speaking, these factors tend to be grouped into three categories each correspond to a
stage in the life of the wine:

## Primary Aromas

These aromas come directly from the grape variety and the terroir (the environment where the grapes are grown).

| **Source** |               **Aroma Family**                | **Examples**                                                |
| :--------: | :-------------------------------------------: | :---------------------------------------------------------- |
|   Grape    |   <Aroma type="citrus">Citrus Fruit</Aroma>   | Lemon, Orange, Grapefruit                                   |
|   Grape    | <Aroma type="tree-fruit">Green Fruit</Aroma>  | Apple, Pear, Gooseberry, Grape                              |
|   Grape    |   <Aroma type="red-fruit">Red Fruit</Aroma>   | Strawberry, Raspberry, Red Currant                          |
|   Grape    | <Aroma type="black-fruit">Black Fruit</Aroma> | Plum, Blackberry, Black Currant, Blueberry                  |
|   Grape    | <Aroma type="stone-fruit">Stone Fruit</Aroma> | Peach, Apricot                                              |
|   Grape    | <Aroma type="tropical">Tropical Fruit</Aroma> | Pineapple, Mango, Banana                                    |
|   Grape    |      <Aroma type="floral">Floral</Aroma>      | Blossom, Rose, Violet                                       |
|   Grape    |      <Aroma type="herbal">Herbal</Aroma>      | Mint, Eucalyptus, Thyme, Rosemary, Fennel,Licorice          |
|   Grape    |     <Aroma type="vegetal">Vegetal</Aroma>     | Bell Pepper, Grass, Tomato Leaf, Asparagus, Olive, Beetroot |
|   Grape    |      <Aroma type="earthy">Earthy</Aroma>      | Stone, Minerals                                             |

## Secondary Aromas

These aromas develop during the winemaking process, primarily through fermentation and due to the influence of oak barrels.

|       **Source**        |           **Aroma Family**            | **Examples**                                         |
| :---------------------: | :-----------------------------------: | :--------------------------------------------------- |
| Alcoholic Fermentation  | <Aroma type="microbio">Yeasty</Aroma> | Biscuit, Bread, Toasted Bread, Dough, Cheese, Yogurt |
| Malolactic Fermentation | <Aroma type="microbio">Creamy</Aroma> | Butter, Cheese, Cream                                |
|           Oak           |    <Aroma type="oaky">Oaky</Aroma>    | Vanilla, Coconut, Smoke, Clove, Cedar, Sawdust       |
|      Oak Toasting       |  <Aroma type="earthy">Earthy</Aroma>  | Smoke                                                |

## Tertiary Aromas

These aromas develop as the wine matures in the bottle or in oak barrels over an extended period of time.

|     **Source**     |             **Aroma Family**              | **Examples**                           |
| :----------------: | :---------------------------------------: | :------------------------------------- |
|     Bottle Age     |    <Aroma type="earthy">Earthy</Aroma>    | Leather, Earth Mushroom, Forest Floor  |
|     Bottle Age     |    <Aroma type="animal">Animal</Aroma>    | Umami, Game, Meat, Soy Sauce, Balsamic |
|     Bottle Age     |      <Aroma type="oaky">Oaky</Aroma>      | Chocolate, Coffee, Tobacco             |
| Bottle Age (white) |    <Aroma type="earthy">Earthy</Aroma>    | Honey                                  |
|   Oxidative Age    | <Aroma type="raisin"> Dried Fruit</Aroma> | Raisin, Fig, Prune                     |
|   Oxidative Age    | <Aroma type="raisin"> Dried Fruit</Aroma> | Raisin, Fig, Prune                     |
|   Oxidative Age    |    <Aroma type="ageing">Nutty</Aroma>     | Almond, Hazelnut, Walnut               |
|   Oxidative Age    |      <Aroma type="oaky">Oaky</Aroma>      | Caramel                                |
